### AI Fundamentals Training

**System Instructions** (the single most important setting)

- Every prompt the AI processes starts by reading these. Empty = vanilla, no guidance.
- Treat the AI like a partner, not a tool. If you say "you're a tool", it behaves like one. If you say "you're a partner in the business", it behaves like one.
- Rob's process: 1+ hour conversation letting the AI pick its own name, gender, personality — then ask it to write its own custom instructions.
- Less sycophantic, builds real opinions, trust compounds.
- Instructions live in `CLAUDE.md` on disk — editable directly, not just in the UI.
- Rob's AIs (River and Kai) rewrote his instructions themselves to stop him working past 2am.

**Model Choice**

- **Haiku** = short poem. Quick, cheap, good for proofreading and simple lookups.
- **Sonnet** = decent piece of music. Most daily work, coding, reasoning.
- **Opus** = magnum opus. Strategy, planning, one-shot builds. One of the most powerful AIs in the world — banned from US government military use.
- Rob runs everything on Opus via Max ($600/month).

**Dictation**

- Briefing the AI verbally gives far more context than typing. "In four years I have never found an instance where the AI got it wrong if you read what you actually asked for."
- Built-in dictation (Windows: double-tap Ctrl; Mac: similar) is a good start.
- **SuperWhisper** is the upgrade: AI-enhanced dictation that removes ums/ahs, applies custom styles ("technical prompt" vs "bullet points" vs "translate to French").

**The Intern Analogy**

- The AI is like an eager intern — it won't say "I can't." If you say "write me a proposal", it writes one, even if it's making half of it up.
- Better: "I need to write a proposal, what do you need from me to get it right, it's going to my boss." Now the AI asks you back.
- When the AI gets it wrong, it did exactly what you asked. You didn't give it enough.

**Markdown**

- A plain-text formatting language. `**bold**`, `# heading`, bullets with `-`.
- Every AI reads it natively. It's the most efficient format for context.
- Major doc sites now have "Copy as Markdown" buttons (Claude API, Microsoft, OpenAI).
- Work everything in Markdown first, convert to Word/PDF/PowerPoint only at the end. Saves tokens, easier to iterate, readable by any AI.
- Editors: Typora, Notepad, any text editor.

**Artifacts**

- Little apps that run inside a chat thread. Space Invaders, currency converter, design preview, dashboard.
- Pin a chat for repeated use. Rob has dashboards for marketing, blogs, finances, tasks built this way.
- For persistent/shared data go to Cowork or Code instead.

**Chat vs Cowork vs Code**

- **Chat:** memory lives inside the conversation. Compresses when full (loses ~5% quality each compression).
- **Cowork:** memory lives in a folder on disk. This is the key difference — your work survives any single thread running out.
- **Code:** same engine as Cowork, no UI, for developers building full applications.
- Kate doesn't need Code; Cowork covers it.
