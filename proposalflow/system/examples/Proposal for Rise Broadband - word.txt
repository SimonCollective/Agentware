Rise Broadband AI Discovery Engagement Proposal

Executive summary

Rise Broadband is the largest fixed‑wireless broadband provider in the
United States, employing more than 600 people and serving residential
and business customers in 16 states. The company provides internet and
voice services with speeds ranging from 3 Mbps to 1 Gbps and has been
rated Best Rural ISP for four consecutive years.
SoftHouse Advisory is a global AI consultancy serving clients across the
US, Canada, the UK, and Europe. With a team of experienced AI
consultants and over 200 developers, SoftHouse helps organizations
progress from early steps in generative AI adoption to becoming fully
AI-forward, sustainable enterprises.

This proposal outlines a four‑week Real‑Time Discovery engagement
designed to help Rise identify and prioritize AI opportunities across
its network, data and customer‑service domains. Our goal is to deliver a
clear roadmap and a high‑impact pilot that can unlock efficiency, reduce
costs and improve service quality using generative and agentic AI.

Objectives

Understand Rise’s Customer and Network landscape. Working with key
stakeholders we will map and understand Rise’s current network and
customer workflows, datasets, processes, and systems.

Identify high‑impact AI opportunities. Using this understanding we will
pinpoint both quick‑win and strategic applications of AI across this
landscape, for example, reducing the cost of technical support,
implementing predictive maintenance, technician dispatch optimization,
generative call summarization and proactive outage detection.

Assess AI readiness. An assessment of data availability, governance,
compliance and change‑management readiness will highlight enablers and
gaps. We will draw on recognized frameworks (e.g., NIST AI RMF 1.0) to
recommend governance and risk‑mitigation practices if needed.

Deliver a prioritized roadmap and pilot plan. Findings will be
synthesized into a discovery report containing an AI opportunity matrix,
high‑level solution architectures, estimated ROI and a series of pilot
proposals

Deliverables

1.  Discovery report summarizing:
    a.  AI opportunity matrix, list of use cases across network,
        customer and support functions ranked by impact and feasibility.
    b.  High‑level solution architectures, for top use cases.

2.  Data and technology readiness assessment with recommendations for
    governance, privacy and security aligned to relevant standards.

3.  Change‑management and training plan to uplift skills and encourage
    adoption.

4.  Estimated ROI and timeline for top initiatives to help prioritize
    investments.

5.  Executive presentation summarizing key findings, recommendations and
    pilot details.

Investment

covers a team comprising a Lead Consultant, Process Specialist, Product
Manager and AI Engineer as required. The engagement will be intensive

Why AI now?

Businesses across the world have demonstrated that embracing generative
and agentic AI can deliver dramatic productivity gains and cost savings:

-   Salesforce reported that their internal AI tools are currently doing
    around 30% to 50% of the company’s work [1].
-   Microsoft COO States the company has already saved $500M by
    embracing AI [2].
-   Walmart integrated agentic AI into support and development; during
    its Q4‑2025 earnings call the company said AI coding tools saved
    about 4 million hours [3].
-   Klarna CEO Sebastian Siemiatkowski revealed that AI adoption enabled
    the fintech to reduce headcount from about 5 000 to 3 000 employees,
    with their AI assistant performing the work of 700 customer‑service
    agents[4].
-   Centro de la Familia used AI to automate reporting; yielding a
    five‑fold reduction in admin time and 54% cost reduction, turning
    hours‑long tasks into minutes[5].
-   A European telecom call‑centre applied generative AI to summarise
    calls and flag churn risks delivering 30% savings in pre and post
    call operations and more than US$5 million in annual operational
    improvements[6].

These examples illustrate how AI can deliver significant efficiencies
and free staff to focus on higher‑value tasks. For a mid‑sized provider
like Rise, similar benefits are attainable.
