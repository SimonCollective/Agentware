Doxim Draft Proposal

1. Executive Summary

Doxim is a leading provider of Customer Communications Management (CCM),
data-driven print, and document automation solutions, primarily serving
the financial services sector (e.g., credit unions, banks, wealth
management). Through acquisitions, Doxim has assembled a collection of
platforms and systems that handle everything from data ingestion
(pre-composed or raw data) to document composition, printing, mailing,
and archival. There are also additional product lines, including payment
solutions and software supporting loan/account origination.

Doxim’s core challenges—high-volume manual QA, repetitive professional
services activities, siloed or legacy platforms, and specialized custom
implementations—offer substantial opportunities for generative AI and
automation. By integrating AI-driven solutions, Doxim can:

-   Reduce onboarding time and cost for new or existing clients

-   Automate data QA and anomaly detection for ongoing production

-   Accelerate document composition and transformation during
    implementations

-   Improve client services efficiency by applying generative AI for
    ticket triage, knowledge management, and self-service

-   Lay the groundwork for faster, more scalable migrations of clients
    onto strategic, modernized CCM platforms

The following proposal details a multi-phase engagement to assess,
design, and implement Gen AI initiatives, with measurable KPIs such as
“man-hours per client onboarded”,“QA automation coverage” and “delivery
speed”. We will start with an “arms-length co-pilot” approach for key
teams (professional services, data processing) and advance through
increasing levels of automation, ultimately delivering transformative
gains in operational efficiency and margin.

2.1 About Doxim

Core Offering: Document composition, print & mail, digital delivery,
payment services, and software-based solutions for BFSI, with a heavy
emphasis on data processing and custom implementation for each client.

Professional Services & Data Ops: Large teams handle thousands of

to verifying data integrity.

Multiple Platforms: Legacy and modern CCM engines across geographies,

3. Current Pain Points

From the discovery call, Doxim’s main pain points fall in these areas:

Professional Services / Implementation

-   High volume of repetitive tasks when onboarding new customers or
    adding features

-   Building out composition templates (markup languages, multi-language
    code)

-   Mapping raw data to final output (transaction statements, notices)

-   Manual QA of changes and partial rework

  Data Operations

-   Pre-Processing QA: Data can arrive incomplete or incorrect; manual
    checks are time-consuming

-   Post-Production QA: Validating final output involves numerous steps,
    sometimes a bottleneck for final delivery

  Legacy & Multiple Platforms

-   Acquisitions have created multiple CCM composition engines;
    transferring a client from one to another is large and expensive

-   Minimizing overhead from multiple codebases, languages, and
    specialized staff

  Client Service & Support

-   Teams handle thousands of service tickets and changes each year

-   Potential for AI in routing tickets, summarizing issues, and
    eventually auto-resolving common requests

  Security and Privacy Concerns

-   Strict data confidentiality rules require solutions that do not send
    PII or statements to public LLMs

-   Necessitates a privacy-conscious approach (e.g., “no data leaves the
    facility,” synthetic data approaches, or on-premise LLM solutions)

4. Strengths of AI & Potential Solutions

Generative AI can help Doxim:

-   Accelerate Onboarding:

    -   AI-driven data mapping & transformation code generation: The
        model creates Python/.Net scripts to handle new data formats
        quickly.

    -   AI-based QA prompts: Automated generation of test plans and test
        data.

-   Improve Data QA:

    -   Pre-processing checks for file anomalies (e.g., missing headers,
        zero-length data). AI can learn from historical client data to
        predict likely data errors.

    -   Post-composition checks that compare final documents with
        expected templates or known baselines, highlighting anomalies.

-   Migrate Legacy Platforms:

    -   AI can parse existing composition templates, then auto-generate
        equivalent templates in a new CCM engine.

-   Client Services Efficiency:

    -   Generative AI co-pilot to draft updates, knowledge base
        articles, or route tickets to the correct product team.

    -   Potential future: partial or full self-service for
        straightforward queries (reducing the load on 50+ support
        agents).

-   Continuous Improvement & Measurement:

    -   By measuring KPIs such as “Average man-hours needed to onboard a
        client,” Doxim can track how effectively AI-driven automation
        reduces labor cost.

5. Recommended Approach

5.1 Overview

A multi-phase approach will ensure Doxim can methodically discover and
prove value, then scale AI solutions across professional services, data
ops, support, and beyond.

1.  Phase 1: AI Readiness & Discovery

    a.  Discovery & Mapping: Map out Doxim’s main processes for
        onboarding, data QA, ticket handling, and across the multiple
        CCM platforms.

    b.  Data Architecture & Security: Identify potential data
        constraints (PII, on-prem requirements) and outline best
        practices.

    c.  Stakeholder Education & Brainstorming: Short workshop(s) to
        align on AI fundamentals, clarify scope, and gather targeted use
        cases from professional services, data ops, and client support.

2.  Phase 2: Prototyping & Quick Wins

    a.  Pilot #1 – “Arms-Length Co-Pilot”:

        i.  Implement a secure environment (e.g., GPT in Azure or a
            private LLM) for a professional services co-pilot that
            drafts transformation scripts or composition templates.

        ii. Evaluate gains in reduced dev/QA hours.

    b.  Pilot #2 – “QA Automation”:

        i.  Use generative AI to compare pre- vs. post-composition data
            sets.

        ii. Auto-generate detailed QA test cases.

    c.  Measure ROI early to refine approach.

3.  Phase 3: Broad Implementation & AI Tools Integration

    a.  Rollout successful pilots to the full professional services and
        data ops organizations.

    b.  Extend AI usage to client service desk for routing and knowledge
        base.

    c.  Consolidate approaches for template-building and code generation
        across the legacy CCM engines.

    d.  Implement metrics-driven approach (e.g., man-hours per new
        client, ticket resolution time, etc.) to continuously measure
        efficiency gains.

4.  Phase 4: Advanced Co-Pilot & Migration Strategies

    a.  AI as Primary Operator: Let AI propose or even directly apply
        data transformations for new clients, requiring only final human
        approval.

    b.  Legacy Platform Migration: Use AI to parse old composition
        definitions and automatically replicate them in the newer Doxim
        platform(s).

    c.  Full or Near-Full Automation: For stable, repetitive tasks, the
        AI pipeline can run end-to-end with minimal human input.

5.2 Staged Process for AI Automation

Below is the maturity model that Doxim can leverage to expand AI’s role
methodically:

1.  Arms-Length Co-Pilot

    a.  Key individuals get AI-driven training and suggestions to speed
        coding, analysis, QA.

    b.  AI does not automatically transform or import data—staff remain
        the gatekeepers.

    c.  Goals: Build trust, measure initial efficiency gains, upskill
        employees.

2.  True Co-Pilot

    a.  AI becomes a daily collaborator: it auto-generates scripts, maps
        data, and suggests next steps.

    b.  Humans correct or fine-tune results, eventually implementing
        them.

    c.  Goals: Larger reduction in average hours, faster TTR
        (time-to-resolution) for new client setups.

3.  AI as Primary Operator

    a.  AI attempts to handle all standard or well-defined
        transformations, requiring only final approval.

    b.  Real-time QA and anomaly detection integrated, with minimal
        human monitoring.

    c.  Goals: Large automation coverage, improved accuracy through
        continuous learning.

4.  Full Automation

    a.  AI fully manages repetitive or stable tasks end-to-end, with
        confidence thresholds to escalate anomalies.

    b.  QA becomes a monitoring function, focusing on edge cases or new
        scenarios.

    c.  Goals: Scale massively with minimal marginal cost, redeploy
        staff to higher-value tasks.

6. Proposed Phases in Detail

Phase 1: AI Readiness & Discovery

-   Activities:

    -   Stakeholder interviews with professional services, data ops, and
        support leads.

    -   Technical review of existing platforms, data flows, data
        security constraints.

    -   AI Education Workshop with key management & staff.

    -   Detailed scoping of pilot opportunities (onboarding vs. QA vs.
        client support).

-   Deliverables:

    -   AI Readiness Report: Data flow diagrams, security approach,
        recommended pilot scope.

    -   Prioritized list of 2–3 pilot initiatives with ROI estimates.

Phase 2: Prototyping & Quick Wins

-   Activities:

    -   Configure or expand a secure AI environment (Azure OpenAI,
        private LLM, etc.).

    -   Pilot for arms-length co-pilot in professional services
        (auto-generate data transformation scripts, composition
        templates).

    -   Pilot QA automation approach (pre- vs. post-composition checks).

    -   Evaluate man-hours saved, throughput, error reduction.

-   Deliverables:

    -   Working pilot solutions with documented results.

    -   An initial partial rollout to a subset of the team.

    -   ROI & Next Step Recommendations for full implementation.

Phase 3: Broad Implementation & AI Tools Integration

-   Activities:

    -   Extend pilot solutions to all relevant teams in professional
        services, data ops, and possibly client support.

    -   Set up usage metrics: number of hours saved, TTR, etc.

    -   Potential integration with ticketing systems (for auto-routing
        or response drafting).

-   Deliverables:

    -   Deployed AI-based automation (co-pilot style) organization-wide.

    -   Clear measurement framework tracking man-hour savings,
        speed-to-onboard, etc.

    -   Ongoing knowledge transfer to Doxim staff.

Phase 4: Advanced Co-Pilot, Migration, & Full Automation

-   Activities:

    -   Expand from “true co-pilot” to “AI as primary operator” for
        well-defined transformations.

    -   Work on partial or full automatic platform migration (legacy
        templates to new CCM).

    -   Final stage: fully automated tasks for stable processes.

-   Deliverables:

    -   Scripts & pipelines that handle data transformation or template
        creation automatically.

    -   Policies & confidence thresholds for minimal human QA.

    -   Roadmap for “fully automated” tasks, letting staff focus on
        high-level innovation.

7. Success Metrics & ROI

-   Man-Hours per Client Onboarded

    -   Baseline: e.g., “X hours per typical project”

    -   Target: reduce by 30–50% within 12–18 months

-   QA Automation Coverage

    -   Baseline: manual checks for 80% of data sets

    -   Target: AI-based checks handle 50–70% of QA

-   Implementation Throughput

    -   More projects handled by the same (or smaller) team

    -   Faster time-to-market for new clients

-   Client Satisfaction (internal & external)

    -   Lower error rates, fewer rework cycles, improved on-time
        delivery

-   Support Ticket Efficiency

    -   A measurable reduction in time to route and resolve standard
        tickets
