# Client Brief: System3 - AI-Powered Leadership Assessment Enhancement

## Client Overview
**Company:** System3  
**Representative:** Andrew Case, Chief Revenue Officer  
**Company Age:** ~3.5 years  
**Team Size:** 10 people (5 leadership team members)  
**Product:** Leadership simulation and assessment platform measuring 15 competencies

## Business Context
System3 provides a scientifically-validated leadership simulation that assesses leadership competencies for talent acquisition and development. Founded by Elan Pratzer (former executive search leader at The Caldwell Partners and Korn Ferry), the company addresses the low success rates (50-60%) in C-level transitions.

Andrew Case recently transitioned from board member to Chief Revenue Officer three months ago, marking an inflection point where the company needs to commercialize their scientifically-proven product.

## Current Pain Points & Challenges

### 1. Manual, White-Glove Service Model
- All data interpretation requires IO psychologists or highly trained experts
- Manual synthesis of 15 competency scores into leadership style assessments
- Consultative discovery process to understand business context is entirely manual
- Limited scalability due to expert dependency

### 2. Inconsistency in Expert Delivery
- Andrew's observation: *"If you want to talk about risk, that's risky. It's not really consistent when you look at it."*
- *"We suffer the same thing with the three or four people that give feedback in our company today... there's a high potential for you to deliver suddenly different results and right down to somewhat dramatically different results."*
- Expert bias towards overly positive assessments over time

### 3. Scalability Limitations
- Cannot grow without proportionally increasing expensive expert headcount
- Current business model prevents SaaS transformation
- Andrew stated: *"We're at this inflection point for the organization where we need to make money. We can't just be, hey, this is really smart."*

### 4. Internal Resistance
- Science team (IO psychologists) and compliance actively resisting AI adoption
- Andrew noted: *"Very actively pushing and pumping the brakes on anything AI saying, you know, that this is, this is putting the integrity of our product and service at risk."*
- Team lacks understanding of AI risk mitigation strategies

## What System3 is Looking For

### Primary Objectives
1. **Automated Result Interpretation**: Transform raw competency scores into synthesized leadership style reports without manual expert intervention
2. **Contextual Business Analysis**: Automate the consultative discovery process to understand client business context and leadership requirements

### Business Transformation Goals
- Andrew explained: *"If it works, it makes for enormous efficiencies in our business model... it also changes our go to market strategy in that we could potentially create more of a SaaS product out of system three."*
- Enable scaling without proportional headcount increases
- Transform from service-based to product-based revenue model

### Additional AI Applications Identified
- Interactive AI coaching based on individual assessment results
- Real-time contextual advice for leaders (e.g., meeting preparation based on their competency profile)

## Proposed Solutions Discussed

### 1. Agentic AI Architecture
Rob Williams proposed implementing multiple narrow-focused AIs to address bias concerns:
- *"You can build those AIs that are critics, you can build AIs that are neutral, you can build AIs that are enthusiasts... then you can create an adjudication layer."*
- This would create consistency while avoiding the positive bias seen in human experts

### 2. Risk Mitigation Strategies
- Local LLMs to keep data on-premises
- Multiple validation layers to achieve 97-99% accuracy
- Human-in-the-loop approach for quality assurance
- Rob emphasized: *"It's not necessarily initially about replacing that function, but what it may be about is scaling that function without having to hire more people."*

### 3. Phased Engagement Approach
Robert Sestito suggested:
1. **Discovery Phase**: Architecture and framework development (weeks, not months)
2. **Implementation Phase**: Build and deploy the solution
3. **Alternative Option**: Executive workshop to identify AI opportunities and prioritize based on ROI

### 4. Extended AI Capabilities
Rob proposed an innovative application:
- *"Imagine the ability for an AI to take any question that the leader may ask and then look at the competencies and apply those competencies to that question... What should I bear in mind? What can you raise as concerns?"*
- Andrew confirmed they've already begun experimenting: *"We just, we have been doing that... and getting some interesting results for sure."*

## Key Stakeholder Perspectives

- **Product Development Lead**: *"Very snappy young product development guy who's an engineer... he's all hands in on this, like he loves it"*

### Resistant Stakeholders
