AI Skills & Adoption Uplift (I think this is required regardless of the
below choices)

An internal enablement program focused on AI skills and adoption is
essential to ensure that teams, from PS to support to leadership, are
equipped to leverage AI safely and effectively. The program would blend
targeted training, real-world use cases, and safe practice guidelines,
building confidence and creating advocates to drive adoption. Broad
rollout of the right tools with good training would enable anyone to
build high value tools and automations that can have real impact, and
the snowball effect that can occur as the organization broadly up skills
can be transformational.

Potential Value:

-   Accelerates safe, compliant AI use across the org

-   Increases productivity by embedding AI into daily workflows

-   Builds a culture of innovation and learning

-   Lowers resistance and anxiety around new tech

-   Supports development of AI “power users” and internal champions

-   Ensures regulatory and security guardrails are followed

Potential Measurements / KPIs:

-   Percentage of staff completing AI enablement program

-   Employee AI usage/adoption rates post-training

-   Number of AI-driven process improvements reported

-   Internal satisfaction and feedback scores

-   Reduction in manual, repetitive tasks

-   Frequency of AI-related compliance incidents

Likely Challenges:

Building confidence at scale demands sustained leadership support, clear
comms, and ongoing opportunities for h experimentation. Some may resist
change or fear job impact, so messaging must focus on upskilling and
empowerment. Ensuring security, privacy, and compliance while
democratizing AI access also requires careful planning and oversight.

Special Note

Whilst not overly advertised as such, nor specifically kept secret, this
fundamentally would be a change management program, putting the right
people in front of the teams, building enthusiasm and excitement around
AI, and being there to answer questions and concerns throughout the
process are all critical success factors. Special attention would be
paid to those who fear or are uncomfortable with the technology,
ensuring care and sensitivity helps bring everyone along on the journey.

AI-Accelerated Mid-Market Adoption & Onboarding

Leveraging AI to streamline and accelerate onboarding for mid-market
customers could be transformative, addressing a known friction point
where current implementations feel overly complex and
resource-intensive. By applying AI to extract actionable onboarding
requirements from unstructured data (like meeting notes and client
docs), at the initial stages of the project, and using these insights to
profile the client, and understand the best path to low effort on
boarding (potentially including elements of self on boarding,
self-learning, and streamlined workflows. And by empowering the
mid-market onboarding teams with best inbreed AI tools and training to
automate much of the process. Euna can create a more standardized,
digestible, and scalable onboarding experience. This shift not only
lowers costs for the business but directly aligns with mid-market
expectations for simplicity, speed, and first-time-to-value.

Potential Value:

-   Reduces perceived and actual onboarding complexity for mid-market
    clients

-   Accelerates time to first value, improving client satisfaction

-   Enables repeatable, scalable onboarding “packages”

-   Supports revenue velocity and reduces sales cycle times

-   Frees resources for more complex/enterprise implementations

-   Enhances brand perception as client-centric and modern

Potential Measurements / KPIs:

-   Average time from contract to “first value”

-   Customer satisfaction (CSAT/NPS) during onboarding

-   Percentage of mid-market onboardings using AI-driven process

-   Implementation cost per client (mid-market segment)

-   Reduction in support tickets related to onboarding confusion

-   Revenue recognized within first 90 days post-sale

Likely Challenges:

Successfully standardizing and packaging onboarding for a diverse
mid-market client base is not trivial, AI must have access to historical
data to spot relevant patterns, and unstructured client requirements
must be accurately extracted and classified. Resistance may come from
internal teams who perceive “one size fits all” as lowering quality, or
from clients with genuinely unique needs. There will also be technical
challenges around integrating disparate data sources and ensuring AI
recommendations are trusted and actionable. Finally, change management
is critical, teams must buy in to both the AI approach and the new ways
of working.

AI-Enabled Self-Serve Support, Learning & Triage

Transforming support and learning through AI involves creating powerful
self-serve knowledge bases, intelligent ticket triage, and guided
troubleshooting tools that empower clients to resolve issues
independently and proactively. By expanding beyond onboarding to provide
dynamic, context-aware support resources, Euna can accelerate issue
resolution, reduce support burden, and deliver more proactive and
data-driven experiences.

Potential Value:

-   Frees up support team for higher-complexity queries

-   Improves customer experience by enabling 24/7 self-service

-   Reduces time to resolution for common issues

-   Builds a living knowledge base that improves over time

-   Lowers support cost-to-serve for all clients

-   Boosts customer retention by solving problems faster

Potential Measurements / KPIs:

-   First-contact resolution rate

-   Average ticket response and resolution time

-   Usage rates of self-serve tools/resources

-   Support ticket volume per client segment

-   Client satisfaction scores (post-support)

-   Knowledge base article effectiveness/feedback

Likely Challenges:

Deploying AI-powered support requires a critical mass of high-quality
knowledge assets and process documentation, as well as ongoing tuning to
ensure accuracy and relevance. Change management can be a barrier,
especially if support staff feel their roles are threatened. There is
also the risk of customer frustration if the self-serve experience is
incomplete or clunky, so human backup must remain accessible. Finally,
measurement and improvement loops must be in place to ensure the system
continually evolves with real customer needs.

Implementation Resource Optimizer

AI can help optimize the allocation of professional services resources
by analyzing project complexity, timelines, and required skill sets,
then dynamically matching staff and predicting bottlenecks or overruns.
This enables better utilization, more consistent project outcomes, and
faster revenue recognition.

Potential Value:

-   Increases project throughput and utilization

-   Minimizes project overruns and resource idle time

-   Improves project forecasting and planning

-   Drives consistency in project outcomes across diverse client types

-   Enhances staff satisfaction by aligning skills/interests with work

-   Accelerates backlog reduction and revenue realization

Potential Measurements / KPIs:

-   Resource utilization rates

-   Average project overrun (time/cost)

-   Project backlog size and velocity

-   Number of projects delivered on-time/on-budget

-   Employee satisfaction with project assignments

-   Time to revenue recognition per project

Likely Challenges:

Building a robust resource optimizer depends on high-quality, granular
project and resource data. Variability in project types and legacy
processes may complicate data collection and AI model training. Change
management is also a factor: teams must trust the optimizer’s
recommendations and be willing to adjust legacy workflows. Finally,
continuous monitoring is needed to ensure optimization doesn’t create
new bottlenecks or quality issues.

AI-Driven Onboarding Hub

An AI-powered “golden thread” solution that creates a seamless data flow
from first conversation through onboarding and beyond, aggregating
unstructured and structured data, meeting notes, documentation, project
plans, and communications. This unified, real-time record gives all
stakeholders a shared, transparent view of requirements, progress,
risks, and sentiment, surfacing issues and opportunities early and
making onboarding measurable and repeatable. However, this is much more
than just a list of documents, the hub would use Agentic AI to extract
relevant facts from all documentation and data and then allow relevant
team members to query this fact store in plain english or even build
tools on top of it. Project managers could query customer sentiment, or
the current state of the project timeline, or spot conflicts within the
facts where people have given conflicting information highlighting
potential disconnects. Onboarding engineers could use the data store to
understand requirements, the QA team could use it to build test cases,
and it could be built piece by piece, starting simple and being added to
over time.

Potential Value:

-   Delivers total transparency to all stakeholders

-   Flags potential gaps, risks, or conflicts before they escalate

-   Simplifies project tracking “are there any risks to the delivery
    date”

-   Improves client trust and satisfaction

-   Provides a living record for continuous improvement

-   Enables early intervention to keep projects on track

Potential Measurements / KPIs:

-   Incidents of missed requirements or late-stage surprises

-   Stakeholder satisfaction with onboarding transparency

-   Average time to issue resolution during onboarding

-   Number of proactive interventions triggered by AI

-   Completion rates for onboarding tasks/milestones

-   Reduction in onboarding-related escalations

Likely Challenges:

Aggregating disparate data sources, especially unstructured content, may
require integration and data quality work up front. There may be privacy
or compliance concerns, particularly around meeting transcripts and
sensitive client information. Success also depends on stakeholder
adoption: the system must be seen as an enabler, not extra overhead.
Over time, the AI must continually improve at surfacing relevant
insights while filtering noise.
