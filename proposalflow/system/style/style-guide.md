# Softhouse Proposal Style Guide

## Voice & Tone
- **Professional but human**: blend warmth with credibility
- **Outcome-focused**: emphasize value, ROI, efficiency
- **Consultative**: position as a partner, not just a vendor
- **Action-oriented**: use active voice, verbs tied to business impact

## Formatting
- Use consistent section headers:
  - Title Case for sections
  - Bullets for activities and outcomes
- Use consistent terms (e.g., “Quick Wins,” “Use Case Portfolio,” “AI Roadmap”)
- Apply bold or italics sparingly to emphasize key phrases

## Visuals
- Include clean tables for:
  - Week-by-week deliverables
  - Fee breakdowns
  - Phase outcomes
- Add call-out boxes or side-notes for disclaimers or strategic commentary

## Opening Note
- Personalized, ideally written from the project lead
- Reference prior engagement or shared goals
- Use a casual, friendly tone

## Use of Estimates
- Be transparent about what is fixed vs. what’s scoped later
- Clarify discovery-driven cost variability
- Optional: note hourly or weekly overage rates

## Deliverable Framing
- Don’t just list – explain **why** each matters
- Tie deliverables to outcomes (e.g., productivity gains, staff enablement, regulatory clarity)

## Reusability
- Structure so proposals can be duplicated and tailored easily
- Use consistent phrases across different client proposals to standardize pitch language

## Language Tips
- Replace jargon with plain language
- Use the term “AI practice” or “AI function” for internal enablement
- Frame MVPs around **value-driving roles** (e.g., BAs, QA analysts)

## Strategic Themes to Reinforce
- Immediate ROI (via quick wins)
- Sustainable internal capability (via roadmap and training)
- PR or industry positioning (e.g., Chief AI Officer branding)



# Verbatim Examples

## 🎯 Voice & Tone

### ✅ Confident, Friendly, Human  
Use plain language and human phrasing—conversational but never casual. You’re a trusted partner with expertise, not a faceless vendor.

> “I’m excited to share our proposal for the AI Integration and Data Consolidation Strategy.  
> This proposal is all about using AI to enhance data integration, insights, and operational efficiency.  
> I genuinely believe this is an incredible opportunity to make a big impact on productivity and efficiency across the company.”

---

### ✅ Outcome-Focused  
Emphasize business value, ROI, and momentum. Don’t just describe activity—frame it in terms of what it unlocks.

> “A high velocity pipeline of delivered initiatives that address prioritized use cases, providing tangible business value and clear ROI.”

> “The goal is to generate an annual impact on profitability of $100,000 each month through a variety of methods, such as increasing win rates, gaining broader market reach, aiding customer retention, or lowering operational costs.”

---

### ✅ Partner-Oriented  
Highlight the collaborative nature of the engagement. Position the team as an extension of the client’s own capability.

> “The retainer model offers significant value. It can be used to position the organization as having a Chief AI Officer, enhancing industry reputation and providing PR benefits. It allows flexibility to develop an AI strategy, including governance, ethics, documentation, policies, and an AI usage framework.”

---

## 📐 Structural Patterns

### 🔸 Phased or Sectioned Delivery  
Break work into logical stages, each with a clear objective and a measurable outcome.

> “The engagement will be structured in three key phases:
>
> - Phase 1: Data Mapping and Understanding  
> - Phase 2: Use Case Discovery and Prioritization  
> - Phase 3: Implementation of Use Cases  
>
> These phases can be run sequentially across the organization or executed in focused areas for faster impact.”

---

### 🔸 Week-by-Week Milestones  
Where relevant, outline a weekly cadence for transparency and confidence.

> “Week 1: Stakeholder interviews, systems audit, and initial quick wins identified  
> Week 2: Deep dives, MVP tech testing, and architectural review  
> Week 3: Roadblock resolution and MVP build kickoff  
> Week 4: Draft roadmap and governance documents  
> Week 5–6: MVP refinement, training, and launch preparation”

---

## 🛠️ Deliverables Framing

Use active phrasing and outcomes-based framing when describing outputs.

> “Use Case Portfolio: A list of prioritized use cases with feasibility and ROI estimates along with high-level estimations of effort and value.”  
>
> “AI Strategy and Governance Documentation: A comprehensive AI strategy including governance, ethical guidelines, documentation policies, and a scalable usage framework.”

---

## 💬 Common Phrases to Reuse

- “Actionable insights”  
- “High-velocity pipeline”  
- “Tangible business value”  
- “Strategic roadmap”  
- “Sustainable AI practice”  
- “Quick-win implementations”  
- “Pilot-to-production scale”  
- “Immediate and long-term value”  
- “Ongoing integration and change management”  
- “AI readiness and maturity assessment”  

---

## 🧑‍💼 Closing Style

End with a confident, collaborative note that reinforces alignment and long-term partnership.

> “I’m really looking forward to chatting more about this and aligning it with your vision for the future.”

---

## 📌 Formatting Notes

- **Use Title Case** for major section headings  
- **Bold** strategic deliverables or outcomes  
- Use checklists or bullet points for clarity  
- Use tables for week-by-week plans, fees, or deliverable overviews  
- Keep paragraph structure clean and scannable—avoid dense blocks of text

## Page Setup

- Paper: US Letter (8.5" x 11")
- Margins: 1" on all sides (top, bottom, left, right)
- Content width: 6.5" (page width minus margins)
- Two document sections: Section 1 for cover page (no header/footer), Section 2 for body (with header/footer)

## Cover Page Layout (Section 1, no header/footer)

The cover page is its own document section with no header or footer. Elements appear in this order, all centered:

1. **Top spacing**: ~30pt blank space (spacer paragraph with space-after: 30pt)
2. **Logo**: SoftHouse logo image, centered, approximately 3" wide
3. **Spacing**: ~10pt after logo
4. **Subtitle**: "S O F T H O U S E   A I   A D V I S O R Y" in letter-spaced 10pt #6B7280. Space after: 4pt
5. **Red divider**: Empty centered paragraph with a bottom border (color: #CF2E2E, size: 6, style: single). Space after: 15pt
6. **Spacing**: ~10pt
7. **Title**: Proposal title in 26pt bold black, centered. Space after: 6pt
8. **Client name**: "For [Client Name]" in 14pt #6B7280, centered. Space after: 4pt
9. **Spacing**: ~10pt
10. **Date**: "March 2026" in 11pt #6B7280, centered. Space after: 2pt
11. **Doc ID**: "ID: SHS_Client_YYYYMMDD-X.X" in 9pt #6B7280, centered. Space after: 2pt
12. **Spacing**: ~20pt
13. **Confidentiality notice**: "This document contains proprietary and confidential material of Softhouse Advisory Systems Inc. Any unauthorized reproduction, use, or disclosure of this material is strictly prohibited." in 8pt italic #6B7280, centered

## Header (Section 2, body pages)

- **Left side**: "SOFTHOUSE AI ADVISORY" in 8pt regular #6B7280 (all uppercase)
- **Right side**: Proposal short name in 8pt italic #6B7280 (e.g. "Rise Broadband AI Retainer", "Doxim AI Enablement"). Use a tab character to separate left and right, with a right-aligned tab stop at the content width
- **Bottom border**: Red line (#CF2E2E), size 4, single style, 4pt space below the text
- No logo in the header. Text only.

## Footer (Section 2, body pages)

- **Left side**: "Confidential" in 8pt regular #6B7280
- **Right side**: "Page X" in 8pt regular #6B7280, where X is the page number. Use a tab character with a right-aligned tab stop
- **Top border**: Light gray line (#CCCCCC), size 2, single style, 4pt space above the text

## Section Headings

- Heading 1: 18pt bold black. No red divider underneath. Let the weight and size carry the hierarchy.
- Heading 2: 14pt bold black. Used for sub-sections, tier names, etc.
- Heading 3: 12pt bold #6B7280 (medium gray). Used for "Deliverables", "Timeline", etc.
- Do NOT add red dividers or horizontal rules after headings in the body. The cover page red divider is the only red rule in the document.

## Body Text

- 11pt Calibri, regular weight, black (#000000)
- Space after each paragraph: 6pt
- Between paragraphs (where visual breathing room is needed), insert an empty paragraph with space-after of 2pt to 4pt
- Personal opening note: same body text styling, addressed to the client contact by first name. Sign off with "Best," then "Rob" on the next line. No bold on the name in the opening note.
- Numbered lists and bullet lists: use proper Word list formatting (not manual numbering)

## Tables

All tables in the proposal must follow this exact format:

**Header row:**
- Background: #000000 (pure black)
- Text: 10pt bold Calibri, white (#FFFFFF)
- Cell padding: comfortable (approx 0.05" all sides)

**Body rows:**
- Background: none (white/transparent). No alternating row shading.
- Text: 10pt Calibri, black (#000000)
- First column: bold (deliverable names, item names)
- Remaining columns: regular weight

**Borders:**
- All borders: thin single lines, auto color (light gray), size 4
- Clean, minimal appearance

**Special table values:**
- "Y" or "Included" in comparison tables: use #27AE60 (green), regular weight
- Em-dash or blank for "not included" values

**Table width:** Full content width (6.5")

## Pricing Callouts

When a dollar amount is displayed as a standalone line (e.g. under a tier heading like "Option A: AI Advisor"), format it as:
- 12pt bold, color #1B4F72 (deep blue)
- Space after: ~3pt
- Example: "$5,000 USD per month"

Do NOT use red or green for pricing. Deep blue only.

## Page Breaks

- Cover page is always page 1 (its own section)
- Personal opening note starts on page 2 (first page of section 2)
- Use page breaks before major new sections where appropriate to avoid orphaned headings
- Tables should not be split across pages if avoidable

## Document ID Convention

Format: `SHS_[ClientSlug]_[YYYYMMDD]-[Version]`

Examples:
- SHS_Rise_20260308-1.0
- SHS_Doxim_20260308-3.0
- SHS_NewClient_20260415-1.0

The version number reflects proposal iterations, not engagement versions.
