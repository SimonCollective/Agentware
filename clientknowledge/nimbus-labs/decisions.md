# Nimbus Labs Decisions

## Log

### 2026-04-02, Scope Phase 1 to positioning and ICP only

- **Decision:** Phase 1 of the engagement covers positioning recommendation and ICP refinement only. Brand, visual identity, and campaign work are deferred to Phase 2.
- **Context:** Celeste asked us to look at "everything marketing" after the workshop. Priya wanted speed to a crisp story before Series B conversations start.
- **Options considered:** (a) Full marketing audit, 8 to 10 weeks. (b) Positioning plus ICP, 4 to 6 weeks. (c) Positioning only, 3 weeks.
- **Rationale:** Option (b) lets us deliver something decision-useful before the website refresh in late May, while staying inside Celeste's current budget authority.
- **Who decided:** Us, confirmed by Celeste; noted to Priya.
- **Implications:** We are not responsible for copy, design, or paid channel work in Phase 1. If Celeste brings those in scope, we revisit the SOW.
- **Revisit when:** Phase 1 delivery complete, or sooner if VP Sales hire changes priorities.
- **Sources:** 2026-04-02_scoping-call.md

### 2026-03-12, Anchor positioning on "operational analytics"

- **Decision:** Positioning recommendation will be built around the "operational analytics" category claim rather than "BI" or "embedded analytics."
- **Context:** Workshop exercise tested five framings with the leadership team. "Operational analytics" was the only one all three founders could defend in their own words.
- **Options considered:** "BI for SaaS ops" (rejected: crowded, undifferentiated). "Revenue analytics" (rejected: too narrow, cuts ICP). "Embedded analytics" (rejected: technical framing that confuses buyers). "Operational intelligence" (rejected: overlaps with IT monitoring tools).
- **Rationale:** It is the only frame that mirrors how their best customers already describe their own need, per the interviews.
- **Who decided:** Joint, with Priya, Finn, Celeste, and us.
- **Implications:** Our recommendation deck, the website refresh, and all Phase 1 content anchors on this language. A category claim requires ecosystem validation, which is an ongoing watch item.
- **Revisit when:** Industry press fails to pick up the language by Q4 2026, or a well-funded competitor claims an adjacent label.
- **Sources:** 2026-03-12_positioning-workshop-notes.md
