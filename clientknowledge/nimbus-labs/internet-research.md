# Nimbus Labs: Public-information research

## 1. Snapshot

Nimbus Labs is a B2B operational analytics platform founded in Manchester in 2022 by Priya Chandra and Finn Oakley. The product targets mid-market SaaS companies (roughly 100 to 1,000 employees) and sits between traditional BI and embedded analytics. Headcount is approximately 45 as of Q1 2026, following a Series A in February 2026.

## 2. Ownership and funding

- Seed round: $2.8M, June 2023, led by LocalGlobe (source 1).
- Series A: $12M, February 2026, led by Notion Capital, with participation from LocalGlobe and Angular Ventures (source 2).
- Ownership: founder-led, approximately 55% combined Priya and Finn per Companies House filings (source 3).
- Public filings: accounts filed through 2024. 2025 accounts due July 2026.

## 3. Leadership and key people

- **Priya Chandra, CEO and co-founder.** Ex-Palantir (engineering, 2016 to 2019), ex-GoCardless (head of data, 2019 to 2022) (source 4).
- **Finn Oakley, CTO and co-founder.** Ex-Stripe (infra, 2018 to 2022) (source 4).
- **Celeste Moreau, VP Marketing.** Joined January 2026 from Hotjar, where she led product marketing for three years (source 5).
- No VP of Sales listed. LinkedIn job post for VP Sales opened 2026-04-08, suggesting search in progress (source 6). Flag for key-people.md.

## 4. Products and services

One product: Nimbus Operational Analytics. Sold as a SaaS subscription. Pricing listed as "contact sales" on the website, suggesting enterprise sales motion. Customer stories on their site name three companies (Charly, Pillar, and Lumin); all three are mid-market SaaS (source 7).

Claim (source 7): "The only analytics platform built for SaaS operations teams." Flagged as marketing claim; competitors include Mixpanel, Amplitude, and newer entrants like Hex and Equals. Worth testing in positioning work.

## 5. Market and competition

Mid-market SaaS analytics is crowded. Named competitors in public materials and press: Mixpanel, Amplitude, Hex, Equals, ThoughtSpot. Nimbus differentiates on (claim) "operational" framing versus "product" or "BI" framing. Evidence for the claim is thin in public materials; most feature comparisons focus on integrations and pricing.

## 6. Recent signals (last 12 months)

- 2025-08: Launched Salesforce and HubSpot integrations (source 8).
- 2025-11: First mentioned "operational analytics" category framing in a Priya blog post (source 9). Inference: they have been seeding this language publicly for about six months.
- 2026-02: Series A announcement (source 2).
- 2026-03: Celeste Moreau's first public post framed their audience as "revenue and ops leaders" rather than "data teams" (source 10).
- 2026-04: VP Sales search opened (source 6).

## 7. Risks and watch items

- **Customer concentration.** Their top customer stories are three names that appear frequently in case studies and on the website. If revenue concentration is high, the Series B narrative is fragile. Worth asking.
- **Category crowding.** "Operational analytics" is a claim, not a recognized category. Claim-driven categories work when the ecosystem amplifies them; the last 12 months show only Nimbus using the term.
- **Leadership coverage.** No VP Sales and a first-time VP Marketing. A missed hire here could stall post Series A growth.
- **Competitive overlap with Hex.** Hex has been moving into operational use cases and has better developer brand recognition. Worth a head-to-head comparison.

## 8. Open questions

- What is their net revenue retention for enterprise accounts?
- Is "operational analytics" being adopted externally, or is it still a Nimbus-only phrase?
- How committed is Finn to the marketing investment? The CTO voice on positioning matters.
- Are they planning a Series B, and on what timeline?
